People come,
people go.
Life moves in slow spirals.
Scratches on the floorboards—
not just marks,
but murmurs from the past.
You step — the creak beneath your feet
is an embarrassing sound,
which once was an ideal
with an
ambitious dream.
From between the cracks,
grievances leak like
forgotten water.
That raised plank?
Rotten tea,
never wiped away.
In the shadow—
a black cat your mother loved
a decade ago.
The traces look...
NUCLEAR.
But it was…
ONLY
TIME
now.
you stand above it all,
breathing in the silence
after the blast.
